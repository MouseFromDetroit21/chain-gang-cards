CHAIN GANG POKER

A five-card draw game played against a shared community board. You use 2 of your hole cards, one pair from the board, and one single to make your best 5-card hand. At the end you declare whether you're going for the HIGH pot, LOW pot, or both with a SWING. The pot splits between the best high hand and the best qualifying low hand.

A four-card draw game where the goal is to build a "badugi" ‚Äî four cards of completely different suits AND different ranks. No community board, no swing ‚Äî just you, your four cards, and three chances to draw. Declare HIGH or LOW. Only players with a valid four-card badugi can win their pot.

STRATEGY TIPS
üéØ

SUIT PRIORITY

On draw 1 fix duplicate suits before duplicate ranks. A suit conflict is harder to resolve ‚Äî you need exactly one of each.

üìâ

GO LOW

Low badugis are more valuable because they're harder to make. If you hold A-2-3 of different suits protect those and chase the 4th.

üÉè

DRAW TIMING

Use draw 1 for major repairs (up to 3). Use draw 2 for fine-tuning (1-2). By draw 3 you should only need 1 card at most.

üí∞

ROLLOVER POTS

Big rollover pots change the game. Play tighter and wait for a strong badugi before committing chips into a massive pot.
